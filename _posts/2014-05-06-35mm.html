I haven't updated my blog in two months, sorry.

I should probably have written about my Blue Mountains trip in March (it's still a draft) and my mid semester break trip to Melbourne and Tasmania which happened two weeks ago, but I'll save those for a day when I'm up for it. I sort of recently bought a new camera, because two obviously isn't enough. I decided to invest in an analog camera. I was given a project to do in my Photogram to Instagram class which required the use of 35mm film photography, which I had no clue about.  I found out it is essentially the same thing as shooting with my DSLR in manual mode, all I had to do was learn to load a roll of film.  I picked up my Nikon FM10 from a store in Newton called Smile Please which specializes in analog and vintage cameras.


 

I started out shooting with black and white film (Kodak Tri-X 400) because that was what was required for class. Also, the College of Fine Arts has resources for developing your own B&W film which was very exciting because the digital process is not this extensive. Yesterday, I finally learned how to scan film with a flatbed (Epson V700), although I still need to learn to use a real film scanner such as the Hasselblads the school offers.
